Domain

Research Domain

Explore the scientific foundation, research gaps, and technical framework driving the Ocean Sense project.

01

Literature Survey

Synthesis of MAC protocol evolution, energy harvesting systems, QoS provisioning, and LoRa-based marine IoT applications.

📡

Evolution of MAC Protocols in UWSNs

Traditional underwater MAC protocols (FAMA, MACA-U) rely on acoustic communication with long propagation delays. Recent research has shifted toward adapting terrestrial LPWAN technologies—especially LoRa—for near-surface marine monitoring. LoRa's Chirp Spread Spectrum (CSS) modulation provides excellent link budget and robustness with minimal transmission power.

⚡

Energy Harvesting & MAC Protocols

Energy Harvesting MAC (EH-MAC) protocols achieve Energy Neutral Operation (ENO), where average harvested energy matches or exceeds consumption. Modern approaches employ predictive energy management with adaptive duty cycling using EWMA-based solar forecasting and wave energy estimation. Cross-layer integration between energy subsystem and MAC layer is essential for optimal performance.

🎯

QoS Provisioning in LoRa Networks

Standard LoRaWAN treats all packets as best-effort, lacking priority differentiation. Recent studies propose priority queuing, parameter manipulation (Spreading Factor adjustment), and reinforcement learning approaches. A critical gap exists in coupling Energy Harvesting with QoS requirements, where high reliability often demands high energy consumption.

🌊

Marine Debris Detection & Tracking

Marine plastic pollution is an escalating crisis with millions of metric tons entering oceans annually. Current monitoring relies on sporadic surveys, satellite imagery, and UAVs—all limited by temporal resolution and operational costs. A distributed IoT swarm architecture can provide continuous, in-situ monitoring with real-time detection and drift pattern tracking.

02

Research Gap

Three Critical Gaps Identified:

Gap 1 - Unified Cross-Layer EH-MAC Design: Energy Harvesting and MAC scheduling are treated as separate optimization domains. No unified framework integrates dual-source (solar + wave) stochastic energy prediction directly into LoRa MAC scheduling logic, leading to suboptimal duty cycling and unreliable data delivery.

Gap 2 - Resilience Under Swarm Mobility: Most LoRa MAC techniques are evaluated for static deployments. Surface buoys drift with currents, pitch with waves, and experience frequent wave-induced occlusion, creating rapid SNR fluctuations and hidden-node scenarios that existing algorithms cannot handle at required temporal scales.

Gap 3 - Practical Validation: Although many studies propose advanced energy prediction techniques, most rely on synthetic datasets or computationally heavy models unsuitable for microcontroller deployment. Lightweight approaches (EWMA, regression) have been suggested but rarely validated on real embedded hardware in stochastic marine environments.

03

Research Problem

Energy-neutral communication with priority-driven data delivery in marine debris monitoring.

❓
"How can a novel cross-layer Energy-Resilient Adaptive Communication (ERAC) protocol be designed and implemented to enable reliable, priority-based data transmission for marine debris monitoring, while ensuring energy neutrality through predictive management of stochastic solar and wave energy sources on resource-constrained LoRa nodes?"

Core Challenge: Deploying marine surface sensor networks for real-time debris monitoring requires simultaneous energy neutrality and priority-driven delivery under highly variable energy availability and intermittent connectivity caused by wave occlusion, mobility, and environmental instability.

04

Research Objectives

Design, develop, and validate the Energy-Resilient Adaptive Communication (ERAC) protocol for LoRa-based marine IoT.

SO1

ERAC Architecture Design

Design a cross-layer MAC architecture that interfaces the physical energy harvesting hardware with the data link layer, enabling dynamic access to State-of-Charge (SoC) and energy generation rates for protocol decision-making.

SO2

Predictive Energy Management

Develop and implement lightweight algorithms for forecasting solar and wave energy availability on embedded microcontrollers (EWMA-based), enabling adaptive duty cycling that prevents energy depletion while maximizing network availability.

SO3

QoS-Aware Scheduling

Design a priority-based packet scheduling mechanism that differentiates between critical debris alerts and routine telemetry, optimizing transmission parameters (Spreading Factor, backoff timers) to guarantee delivery of high-priority data.

SO4

Validation & Implementation

Validate ERAC using NS-3 network simulator (10-15 nodes) under stochastic marine channel conditions, and implement on a hardware prototype (ESP32/Raspberry Pi + SX1276/SX1262 LoRa) to demonstrate real-world feasibility.
