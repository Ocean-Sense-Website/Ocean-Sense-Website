Domain

Research Domain

Explore the scientific foundation, research gaps, and technical framework driving the Ocean Sense project.

01

Literature Survey

A synthesis of existing research on ocean monitoring, IoT in marine environments, and AI-based analytics.

📡

IoT in Marine Environments

Existing studies demonstrate the feasibility of deploying wireless sensor networks in shallow and deep oceanic zones. Challenges include power supply, data transmission latency, and sensor corrosion resistance. Multiple frameworks have been proposed using LoRaWAN and satellite communication.

🧬

Marine Ecosystem Monitoring

Traditional ocean monitoring relies on infrequent manual surveys and moored buoys with limited sensor arrays. Recent literature explores autonomous underwater vehicles (AUVs) and satellite imaging for broader coverage, though real-time analytics capabilities remain limited.

🤖

AI & Machine Learning Applications

Deep learning models including LSTM networks and CNNs have been applied to oceanographic time-series data for anomaly detection. Transfer learning approaches show promise for adapting models across different geographic ocean regions with minimal retraining.

🌡️

Climate Change & Ocean Health

Rising sea temperatures, ocean acidification, and deoxygenation are documented threats to marine biodiversity. Literature highlights urgent needs for continuous, high-resolution monitoring systems capable of detecting early warning signals of ecosystem degradation.

02

Research Gap

Despite significant advances in individual areas of IoT hardware, marine sensing, and AI analytics, no unified, cost-effective platform exists that integrates all three layers seamlessly for real-time ocean monitoring at scale.

Current solutions suffer from: high deployment costs, proprietary data silos, insufficient AI integration for predictive analysis, and lack of open-access dashboards for research communities. Ocean Sense addresses these gaps by providing an end-to-end open-architecture solution.

03

Research Problem

❓
"How can an integrated IoT-AI system be designed and deployed to provide continuous, real-time monitoring of marine ecosystem health parameters, enabling early detection of environmental threats and supporting data-driven conservation decisions?"
04

Research Objectives

01

Design a Scalable IoT Sensor Architecture

Develop a low-power, corrosion-resistant sensor node capable of measuring temperature, salinity, pH, turbidity, and dissolved oxygen in marine environments.

02

Implement Real-Time Data Transmission

Establish a reliable, low-latency communication pipeline from deployed sensors to a cloud-based data processing platform using LoRaWAN and MQTT protocols.

03

Develop AI-Based Anomaly Detection Models

Train machine learning models on historical oceanographic data to detect anomalies, predict pollution events, and identify ecosystem stress indicators with high accuracy.

04

Build an Accessible Analytics Dashboard

Create an intuitive web-based visualization platform for researchers, conservationists, and policymakers to access, analyze, and export real-time and historical ocean data.

05

Methodology

🔍

Phase 1: Requirements Analysis

Literature review, stakeholder interviews, and identification of key ocean health parameters to monitor.

⚙️

Phase 2: System Design

Hardware selection, sensor node prototyping, cloud architecture design, and database schema planning.

🏗️

Phase 3: Development

Agile development of sensor firmware, backend APIs, AI models, and the analytics dashboard frontend.

🧪

Phase 4: Testing & Validation

Lab and field testing of sensor accuracy, system integration tests, AI model validation, and performance benchmarking.
